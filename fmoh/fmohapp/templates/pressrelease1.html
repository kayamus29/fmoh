2025 World Cancer Day: FG to Establish, Upgrade Tertiary Health Institutions for Cancer Care Services

Samuel Anyanwu February 6, 2025
0 Comments 15K Views

In a landmark announcement on World Cancer Day 2025, the Federal Government of Nigeria, through the Ministry of Health, unveiled plans to establish and upgrade six tertiary health institutions with state-of-the-art Radiotherapy and Nuclear Medicine facilities. The initiative aims to significantly boost cancer prevention, early detection, and treatment services across the nation.

During a press briefing at the Ministry’s headquarters in Abuja, Honourable Minister of State for Health & Social Welfare, Dr. Iziaq Adekunle Salako, emphasized that the government has invested more in cancer care over the last 18 months than any previous administration. “This is a transformative step towards ensuring that every Nigerian has access to world-class cancer care,” he stated.

The upgraded facilities will be commissioned in phases—with three centers expected to be operational by May 2025 and the remaining three slated for commissioning on or before the 2026 World Cancer Day. In addition, the Federal Government is set to roll out a Catastrophic Health Insurance plan designed to alleviate the financial burden of cancer treatment for patients, particularly those with advanced conditions.

This initiative aligns with the National Health Sector Renewal Investment Initiative (NHSRII) and builds on previous successes in cancer prevention programs, including widespread HPV vaccination and early detection campaigns. With these measures, the Ministry aims to reduce cancer-related mortality and improve patient outcomes nationwide.

"The establishment and upgrading of these centers will not only enhance cancer care services but also serve as a catalyst for training and research in oncology. Our goal is to ensure that no Nigerian is left behind in the fight against cancer." - Dr. Salako

As the country commemorates World Cancer Day under the theme “United by Unique,” this press release underscores a renewed commitment to tackling cancer through innovative strategies and comprehensive healthcare reforms. Stakeholders from both the public and private sectors are urged to support these initiatives, paving the way for a healthier future for all Nigerians.
