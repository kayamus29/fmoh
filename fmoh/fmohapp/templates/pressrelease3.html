CM Pate Details Nationwide Emergency and Ambulance Programme

Emeka Nwankwo January 28, 2025
8 Comments 25K Views

In a comprehensive briefing earlier this week, the Coordinating Minister of Health, CM Pate, detailed the rollout of a nationwide emergency and ambulance programme designed to revolutionize emergency healthcare delivery across Nigeria. This ambitious initiative aims to ensure rapid response and effective patient transport during critical medical emergencies, particularly in remote and underserved areas.

The programme will see the deployment of modern ambulance fleets equipped with advanced life support systems, as well as the establishment of centralized dispatch centers to streamline emergency response times. "Timely intervention is crucial, and our goal is to leave no Nigerian behind when it comes to accessing emergency care," CM Pate remarked during the briefing.

Alongside these developments, the Ministry is partnering with both public and private sector stakeholders to train healthcare professionals in advanced emergency care protocols and to integrate digital tracking systems that optimize ambulance dispatch operations.

"This initiative marks a significant step forward in transforming our emergency services. With a coordinated approach, we can ensure that every life is given the best chance at survival." - CM Pate

The nationwide rollout is set to commence immediately, with pilot projects already underway in several states. This initiative underlines the Ministry's unwavering commitment to enhancing emergency response capabilities and safeguarding public health across the country.
