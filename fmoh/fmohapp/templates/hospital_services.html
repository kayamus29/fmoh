Hospital Services Division

The Hospital Services Division oversees Teaching Hospitals, Federal Medical Centres, and Specialty Hospitals. These units supervise various Federal Tertiary Hospitals. Recently, the Boards of Management of Federal Tertiary Health Institutions were inaugurated and are now managing the hospitals on a day-to-day basis.

Nursing Division

The Nursing Division oversees nursing activities in the country with the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria as its subordinate regulatory agency. Its functions include:

  • Initiating nursing policies and participating in the planning, formulation, implementation, and coordination of national health policies.
  • Liaising with all state nursing departments in the implementation of planned nursing programmes.
  • Advising the federal government through the Department of Hospital Services.
  • Representing the Federal Ministry of Health in the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria.
  • Initiating and organizing formal courses, national workshops, and seminars for various categories of nurses.
  • Planning, establishing, and maintaining a system of basic, post-basic, and continuing education in areas of need.
  • Responsible for postings and movement of all nurses in the ministry.
  • Initiating and carrying out surveys and research into nursing practice and education for improvement of services.
  • Liaising with the Nursing & Midwifery Council of Nigeria in all its dealings with the ministry.
  • Participating in the appointment, placement, promotion, and discipline of all cadres of nursing and midwives, including the creation of vacancies.

Regulatory and Professional Schools Division

This division oversees Regulatory Bodies, Professional Associations, and Professional Schools that train senior, medium, and junior-level cadres in the Health Sector.

Dentistry Division

The Dentistry Division advises the Federal Government through the Ministry on all Oral Health matters, policy formulation, resource mobilization, monitoring, and evaluation of such policies, supervision of Oral Health in schools, as well as research institutions and delivery of affordable, effective, and qualitative Oral Health Care Services to Nigerians.

National Blood Transfusion Services Division

The core mandate of the National Blood Transfusion Services (NBTS) is to provide a centrally coordinated blood transfusion service on a country-wide basis within the National Health Plan; provision of safe, quality, and adequate blood in an equitable and accessible manner.

Trauma/Medical Emergency Response and Disaster Management Division

This division advises the Federal Government through the Ministry on Health Emergencies and Disaster Management; formulates policies on the same, monitors, and evaluates the implementation of health emergencies and disasters.

e-Health/Telemedicine Programme

The e-Health/Telemedicine Programme encompasses services such as health-related internet information sites, automated online therapy, online consultations (real-time), online pharmacies, tele-health, home monitoring systems, and virtual clinics. This programme aims to develop a national e-Health Policy as well as a National Regulatory Framework with guidelines to standardize e-Health Care Delivery.

National Cancer Control Programme and Nuclear Medicine

The National Cancer Control Programme and Nuclear Medicine advises the Federal Government through the Ministry on Cancer Diseases. It also maintains a Cancer Register in the country and develops cancer policies with necessary monitoring and evaluation functions.
